🚚 COLD CALLING SCRIPT FOR MOVING COMPANIES

1. Intake & Introduction
2. Discovery
3. Close Set
PHASE 1: THE INTAKE & INTRODUCTION
Prospect: "Hello?"
You: "Hello, am I speaking with the owner of {Business Name}?"
Owner: "Yes, this is the owner."
You: "My name is {{Your Name}} with Swift mover Acquisition Group. How are you today?"
(Pause for response)
You: "I am calling because we help local moving companies get more jobs. Are you available to take on more jobs right now, or is your schedule completely full?"
PHASE 2: QUALIFYING & ROADMAP ROUTING
Route A: Account admits "Yes, we definitely need more jobs / we have openings"
"That is exactly why we reached out. What we do is simple: We answer your inquiries in your company name, follow up with missed calls instantly, qualify the leads, and drop booked estimates right into your calendar. That way, you just focus on moving. Would you be open to a quick 10-minute chat later this week to see how we handle it?"
Route B: Account states "We're busy enough / schedule is full"
"That’s a great problem to have, honestly. Out of curiosity, when your crews get completely slammed, does handling every single incoming inquiry fall entirely on you, or do you have a dedicated system just for immediate follow-ups? Even busy companies lose revenue because a hot lead calls, hits voicemail, and immediately dials the next mover on Google. Would it make sense to take a look at how we prevent that, just to keep us in your back pocket?"
PHASE 3: VALUE FRAMEWORK & THE CLOSE
You: "Just to be clear—we aren't a marketing agency trying to sell you expensive ads. We just help you lock down and convert the leads you are already getting. I don't want to eat up your time while you're working. Would Tuesday or Wednesday work better for a quick 10-minute Zoom to see how the system works?"
LIVE OBJECTION SYSTEM WORKBENCH
Objection Check: "How much does it cost?"
Response Structure: "Our plans start right around $300 a month, but it really depends on your fleet size and how many calls you guys bring in. I’d rather take 5 minutes to see how you operate first so I can give you an exact, fair number. Does mid-week work for a quick chat?"
Objection Check: "Just send me some information."
Response Structure: "I can absolutely do that. What’s the best email to send it to? (Get email) Perfect, I'll send over a quick 1-page breakdown today. So it doesn't get lost in your inbox, would it be crazy if I gave you a quick ring on Thursday morning just to see what you thought of it?"

The Clean Close Execution:

"I appreciate your time today. Go crush those operational targets, and I'll look forward to speaking with you soon! Have a great day!"

✅ Customer Information Checklist

✓ Customer Service Rules

  • Smile while speaking.
  • Speak clearly and slowly.
  • Never interrupt the customer.
  • Use the customer's name throughout the conversation.
  • Be patient. Show empathy if the customer seems stressed.
  • Never guess. If you don't know, let them know a team member will follow up.
  • Double-check the spelling of names, emails, and addresses before hanging up.
